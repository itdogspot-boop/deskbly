You bought the perfect chair and desk. But if your setup isn't configured right, you're still risking injury.

Here's how to set up your workstation ergonomically.

## The Golden Rules

1. **Eyes level with top of monitor**
2. **Elbows at 90° when typing**
3. **Feet flat on floor (or footrest)**
4. **Wrists straight, not bent**
5. **20-26" from eyes to screen**

---

## Step 1: Chair Height

Start with your chair.

1. Sit with feet flat on floor
2. Adjust seat height so thighs are parallel to floor
3. Ensure 2-3 fingers fit between seat edge and back of knees
4. Adjust armrests so shoulders are relaxed and elbows are at 90°

---

## Step 2: Desk Height

Your desk should match your chair height.

With elbows at 90°:
- If standing: desk at elbow height
- If sitting: desk should allow elbows at 90°

**Pro tip:** If your desk is too high and can't be adjusted, raise your chair and use a footrest.

---

## Step 3: Monitor Position

This is where most people go wrong.

**Correct position:**
- Top of monitor at eye level
- Monitor 20-26" from eyes (arm's length)
- Monitor centered in front of you
- Tilted slightly back (10-20°)

**If you have multiple monitors:**
- Primary monitor directly in front
- Secondary monitor to the side (at slight angle)
- Both at same height

---

## Step 4: Keyboard and Mouse

- Keyboard centered in front of you
- Mouse right next to keyboard (no reaching)
- Wrists straight, not bent up or down
- Consider a keyboard tray if desk is too high

---

## Step 5: Lighting

- No glare on screen (position perpendicular to windows)
- Task lighting for documents
- Avoid backlighting (window behind monitor = strain)

---

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] Chair height adjusted
- [ ] Feet flat on floor
- [ ] Elbows at 90°
- [ ] Top of monitor at eye level
- [ ] Monitor 20-26" away
- [ ] Keyboard centered
- [ ] Mouse next to keyboard
- [ ] No screen glare

---

## Common Mistakes

**Too low:** Slouching to see screen
**Too high:** Shoulders shrug to type
**Too far:** Leaning forward to see
**Too close:** Strain on eyes

---

## The Bottom Line

A $1,000 chair won't help if your setup is wrong. Take 10 minutes to adjust everything properly.

Your body will thank you at the end of the day.
