If you work from home, your chair is the single most important purchase you'll make. A bad chair will wreck your back in 6 months. A good one lasts a decade.

Here are the 5 chairs actually worth buying in 2026.

## 1. Herman Miller Aeron — Best Overall ($1,395)

Yes, it's expensive. Yes, it's worth it. The Aeron has been the gold standard for 30 years for a reason.

**Why it's #1:**
- Fully adjustable everything (arms, lumbar, tilt, height)
- Mesh back = never gets hot
- 12-year warranty
- Holds its resale value (buy used for $600-700)

**Who should skip it:** Anyone under 5'4" — the size B can feel big. Try the size A.

**Pro tip:** Buy remanufactured from BTOD or Crandall Office. Same chair, $700-800, still has warranty.

---

## 2. Steelcase Leap V2 — Best for Long Sessions ($1,189)

Some people prefer the Leap over the Aeron. It has a padded seat (vs. mesh) and the back flexes with your movement.

**Why it's great:**
- LiveBack technology follows your spine
- More adjustment options than the Aeron
- Padded seat is comfier for all-day use

**Who should skip it:** If you run hot, the padded seat gets warm. Get the Aeron instead.

---

## 3. HON Ignition 2.0 — Best Under $500 ($350)

This is the chair offices buy in bulk — and for good reason. It's not flashy, but it works.

**Why it's great:**
- Adjustable lumbar
- Mesh back, padded seat
- 300 lb capacity
- Looks professional

**Who should skip it:** If you want premium adjustability. The arms are decent but not 4D.

---

## 4. Ticova Ergonomic Chair — Best Under $300 ($249)

The best cheap ergonomic chair we've found. Period.

**Why it's great:**
- Adjustable headrest, lumbar, and arms
- Full mesh (breathable)
- Looks like a $600 chair

**Who should skip it:** Heavy users — the weight capacity is only 250 lb.

---

## 5. IKEA Markus — Best Under $200 ($189)

The Markus is IKEA's best office chair, and at $189 it's hard to argue with.

**Why it's great:**
- High back with headrest
- 10-year warranty
- Simple, clean design
- Available at every IKEA

**Who should skip it:** Anyone who needs adjustable arms. The Markus arms don't move.

---

## Don't Buy These

A quick note on what to avoid:

- **"Gaming chairs"** — 90% are overpriced, poorly designed for actual sitting. Racing bucket seats are for cars, not desks.
- **Amazon chairs under $100** — they fall apart in 6 months. Your back is worth more.
- **Anything without adjustable lumbar** — if you can't adjust the back, it's a stool with delusions.
