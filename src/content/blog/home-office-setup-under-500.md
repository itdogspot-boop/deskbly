## Why These Picks?

### Desk: Fezibo 48" ($159)

Standing desk for $159. It works. Read our [full standing desk comparison](/blog/best-standing-desks-under-300/) for details, but the short version: single motor, 154 lb capacity, gets the job done.

### Chair: Ticova Ergonomic ($249)

This is where we spent the most — and that's intentional. Your chair is the #1 investment. The Ticova has adjustable lumbar, headrest, and armrests. Full mesh so you don't sweat. Feels like a $500+ chair.

### Monitor Light Bar: Quntis ($30)

If you don't have a desk lamp, you're straining your eyes. A monitor light bar costs $30 and clips to your monitor — no desk space used. Massive quality of life upgrade for almost nothing.

### Cable Management ($15)

Two J-channels under the desk + velcro ties. Takes 20 minutes to install, and your setup goes from "mess" to "clean" instantly. No one talks about cable management, but it makes your workspace feel 10x better.

### Desk Mat ($18)

Protects your desk surface, gives you a smooth mouse area, and makes everything look put together. Get a large one (36"x17" or bigger).

### Surge Protector with USB ($20)

You need one anyway. Get one with USB ports built in so you can charge your phone without a separate adapter.

## What About a Monitor?

This budget assumes you're using a laptop or already have a monitor. If you need one:

- **Best budget monitor:** Dell S2422HG 24" — $130-150
- **To fit it in $500:** swap the Ticova for the IKEA Markus ($189) and use the $60 saved toward the monitor

## What NOT to Buy Yet

With $500, skip these for now:

- **Keyboard & mouse** — your laptop's are fine until you earn enough to upgrade
- **Speakers** — use headphones you already own
- **Webcam** — your laptop camera works for now
- **Plants/decor** — nice to have, but earn first

## The Upgrade Path

Once the site starts making money, upgrade in this order:

1. **External monitor** ($150) — biggest productivity boost
2. **Mechanical keyboard** ($50-80) — once you try it, you can't go back
3. **Chair upgrade** ($600-800 used Herman Miller) — the endgame

Start lean. Upgrade with earnings. That's the move.
