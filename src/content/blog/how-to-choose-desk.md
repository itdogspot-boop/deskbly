Buying a desk seems simple. But with so many options — standing, sitting, L-shaped, compact — it's easy to get overwhelmed.

This guide will help you figure out exactly what desk you need.

## Question 1: How Much Space Do You Have?

Before anything else, measure your space.

### Measure Your Room

- **Small room (< 100 sq ft):** Consider a compact desk or wall-mounted
- **Medium room (100-200 sq ft):** Standard 48-60" desk works
- **Large room (200+ sq ft):** L-shaped or expansive desk possible

### Measure Doorways

This is where most people fail. A 60" desk won't fit through a 30" door.

- Measure all doorways the desk must pass through
- Remember: it's easier to carry a desk through doors than to angle it

### Measure Your Work Area

- How much desk surface do you actually need?
- Do you spread out papers? Use multiple monitors?
- Will you have room for a chair to sit comfortably?

**Measure twice, buy once.**

## Question 2: Sitting or Standing?

This is the big decision. Let's break it down.

### Sit-Stand Desks (Standing Desks)

**Best for:**
- People who sit all day and want to switch positions
- Those who want the health benefits of standing
- Workers who want flexibility

**Pros:**
- Switch between sitting and standing throughout the day
- More expensive ($200+)
- Potential health benefits

**Cons:**
- More complex (motors can break)
- Takes adjustment time

### Traditional Sitting Desks

**Best for:**
- Fixed-position workers
- Those on a tight budget
- People who never stand while working

**Pros:**
- Cheaper ($100-300)
- Simpler (no motors)
- More stable

**Cons:**
- No standing option
- Less flexible

### Our Recommendation

**Sit-stand if you can afford it.** The ability to switch positions throughout the day is valuable for both health and productivity.

But if you're on a tight budget or know you'll never use the standing feature, a traditional desk is fine.

## Question 3: What Will You Use It For?

Your use case matters more than you think.

### Laptop Only

If you're just using a laptop, you don't need a huge desk. A compact desk (36-40") works perfectly.

### Dual Monitors

You'll want at least a 55-60" desk to comfortably fit two monitors without crowding.

### Gaming

Gaming setups need:
- Wide desk (60"+) for multiple monitors
- Cable management for all the wires
- Room for keyboard, mouse, and accessories

### Work + Personal

Consider an L-shaped desk or a desk with storage to separate work and personal items.

### Small Apartment

Wall-mounted or corner desks maximize limited space.

### Here's a Quick Reference Table:

| Use Case | Best Desk Type |
|----------|---------------|
| Laptop only | Compact desk |
| Dual monitors | 55"+ desk |
| Gaming | Wide desk (60"+) with cable management |
| Work + personal | L-shaped or desk with storage |
| Small apartment | Wall-mounted or corner desk |

## Question 4: What's Your Budget?

Let's be realistic about what you get at each price point.

### Under $100

- Basic desk, no features
- Manual height adjustment only (if any)
- Smaller work surface
- Good for very light use

### $100-200

- Budget standing desk options (single motor)
- Solid sitting desks
- Decent particle board or engineered wood
- Good for most home offices

### $200-400

- Good standing desks with dual motors
- Quality materials
- More features (memory presets, cable management)
- Great for daily use

### $400+

- Premium everything
- Best motors and stability
- Highest quality materials
- Longest warranties
- For serious home office workers

## Question 5: What Features Matter?

Here's what to look for:

### Must-Haves:

1. **Height range** — Make sure it fits your height (especially important for standing desks)
2. **Weight capacity** — Can it hold your monitor, computer, and accessories?
3. **Stability** — No wobble when typing or at full height

### Nice-to-Haves:

1. **Memory presets** — Save sitting and standing heights
2. **Cable management** — Keep cords tidy and hidden
3. **Drawer/storage** — Organization for supplies
4. **USB ports** — Built-in charging

### Red Flags:

- No weight capacity listed
- Only one motor for a standing desk under $150
- Terrible reviews about wobble
- No warranty

## Quick Decision Guide

Still unsure? Here's a quick decision tree:

### Choose a standing desk if:
- You sit all day
- You want flexibility
- You have the budget ($200+)
- You're concerned about health

### Choose a sitting desk if:
- You only ever sit
- You want simplicity
- You have limited budget
- You'll rarely use the standing feature

### Choose an L-shaped desk if:
- You have corner space to use
- You need dual workstations
- You want maximum surface area

### Choose a compact desk if:
- You have a small room
- You're on a tight budget
- You only need space for a laptop
