Standing desks used to cost $800+. Not anymore. The budget market has exploded, and there are now genuinely good electric standing desks under $300.

But most "best of" lists just throw 15 desks at you with no real comparison. We narrowed it down to 7 that actually deserve your money.

## What We Looked For

- **Motor quality** — single vs. dual motor matters for stability and speed
- **Weight capacity** — if it wobbles with a monitor, it's useless
- **Height range** — needs to work for people 5'2" to 6'4"
- **Build quality** — will it last more than a year?
- **Actual user reviews** — not just spec sheets

## 1. FlexiSpot E7 Basic — Best Overall ($279)

The E7 has been a budget champion for years, and the 2026 version is even better. Dual motor, 355 lb capacity, and a height range of 22.8"–48.4".

**Pros:**
- Dual motor = fast and stable
- 4 memory presets
- Anti-collision detection
- Solid steel frame

**Cons:**
- Desktop sold separately at this price
- Cable management is basic

**Verdict:** If you can only buy one desk, this is it.

---

## 2. UPLIFT V2 Base — Best Frame Only ($259)

If you already have a desktop you love (or want to buy a butcher block countertop from IKEA for $60), the UPLIFT V2 base is unbeatable.

**Pros:**
- Works with any desktop 42"–80" wide
- Extremely stable at all heights
- Advanced keypad with 4 presets

**Cons:**
- No desktop included
- Ships heavy

**Verdict:** Best option if you want to customize your desktop.

---

## 3. Fezibo 48" Electric Standing Desk — Best Budget ($159)

Under $160 for a full desk with motor? The Fezibo delivers. It's not fancy, but it works.

**Pros:**
- Incredible price for what you get
- Comes with desktop
- Splice board design looks decent
- Built-in cable tray

**Cons:**
- Single motor = slower
- 154 lb capacity (enough for most setups)
- Some wobble at max height

**Verdict:** Best for tight budgets. Gets the job done.

---

## 4. SHW 48" Electric Standing Desk — Best for Small Spaces ($149)

Even cheaper than the Fezibo, and surprisingly solid. The SHW is perfect if you don't need a huge surface.

**Pros:**
- Compact footprint
- Under $150
- Simple assembly

**Cons:**
- Basic keypad (no memory presets)
- Not great above 44" height

**Verdict:** If you have a small room and small budget, this works.

---

## 5. Vari Essential — Best Brand Name ($295)

Vari is the brand you see in offices everywhere. Their Essential model brings that quality to the budget range.

**Pros:**
- Brand reliability and warranty
- T-style legs = no crossbar blocking your legs
- Clean design

**Cons:**
- On the higher end of "budget"
- 200 lb capacity (fine, but not best in class)

**Verdict:** Pay a bit more for the peace of mind.

---

## 6. Monoprice Workstream — Best Under $200 ($189)

Monoprice is known for good value electronics, and their desk is no different.

**Pros:**
- Dual motor at this price is rare
- 3 memory presets
- Good cable management

**Cons:**
- Desktop finish scratches easily
- Instructions could be better

**Verdict:** Best dual-motor option under $200.

---

## 7. IKEA BEKANT — Best if You Have IKEA Nearby ($299)

The BEKANT isn't the best on paper, but IKEA's warranty and easy returns make it worth considering.

**Pros:**
- 10-year warranty
- Easy to test in store
- Looks clean

**Cons:**
- No memory presets
- Slower motor
- Only worth it at IKEA price

**Verdict:** Buy it if there's an IKEA near you. Skip the delivery fee.
